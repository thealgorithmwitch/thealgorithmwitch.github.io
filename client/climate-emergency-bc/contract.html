Parties

This Monthly Content Engine Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between Cassandre Arkema, doing business as The Algorithm Witch ("Practitioner" or "Contractor"), and the following client organization ("Client"):

Practitioner
2

Term

The Agreement is a 1-month trial covering a period of 30 calendar days, beginning on the start date confirmed in writing by the parties. If the parties wish to continue after the trial, any extension or renewal must be confirmed in writing.

3

Scope of Work

During the 30-day trial, the Practitioner will provide a monthly content engine focused on content planning, creation support, and light coordination for the Client's public-facing communications.

  • Content creation support.
  • Instagram infographics and carousel content.
  • Bluesky posts.
  • Event invitations or promotional posts as needed.
  • Newsletter support and/or newsletter takeover during the trial.
  • Light strategy and coordination related to those deliverables.
  • The exact monthly volume may be confirmed in writing by email before the trial begins.
4

Deliverables

Deliverables under this Agreement may include draft copy, designed social graphics, carousel layouts, promotional assets, newsletter copy or production support, and coordination communications necessary to move those items through review and publication during the trial period.

The Practitioner will deliver work in digital form using tools and formats reasonably suited to the Client's channels and workflow. Unless otherwise stated in writing, delivery deadlines depend on the Client providing timely source materials, approvals, and access.

5

Payment Terms

The total fee for the 30-day trial is $1,500 USD.

  • $750 USD is due upfront before work begins.
  • $750 USD is due at the end of the 30-day trial.

Invoices are payable in USD by the payment method stated on the invoice. Work does not begin until the upfront payment has been received. Late payments may pause work until the account is brought current.

6

Client Responsibilities

  • Provide timely access to relevant brand materials, platform access, background information, and point-of-contact availability.
  • Review drafts and approvals within a commercially reasonable timeframe so the work can proceed during the 30-day trial.
  • Confirm any required priorities, deadlines, or campaign notes in writing.
  • Secure any rights or permissions needed for materials supplied by the Client.
7

Review and Revisions

Reasonable review and revision cycles are included for work created during the trial, provided the requested changes stay within the agreed scope. Requests that materially expand the scope, volume, or channel mix may require a revised fee or written amendment.

8

Contractor Status

The Practitioner is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer of the Client. The Practitioner retains control over the manner and means of performing the services, subject to the agreed deliverables and schedule requirements.

9

Intellectual Property

Upon full payment of all amounts due under this Agreement, final deliverables created specifically for the Client under this Agreement are assigned to the Client, excluding any pre-existing tools, templates, processes, know-how, or materials owned by the Practitioner. The Practitioner retains ownership of all underlying methods, drafts not selected for final use, and general working processes.

10

Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep confidential any non-public business, operational, or strategic information received from the other party in connection with this Agreement, and to use that information only as necessary to perform or administer the engagement, except where disclosure is required by law.

11

Cancellation / Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement on written notice if the other party materially breaches the Agreement and does not cure the breach within a reasonable period after notice. The Client may also cancel the trial for convenience on written notice, but the upfront payment remains non-refundable once work has begun and the Client remains responsible for payment for work completed through the effective termination date.

12

Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, neither party will be liable to the other for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages arising out of this Agreement. The total liability of the Practitioner under this Agreement will not exceed the total fees actually paid by the Client under this Agreement.

13
