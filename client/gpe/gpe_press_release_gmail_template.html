Atlanta Communities Come Together to Defend Environmental Justice and Climate Resilience

Residents, advocates, elected officials, and public health leaders will gather to highlight community solutions and demand protections as federal rollbacks threaten progress on environmental justice.

MEDIA CONTACT: Girl Plus Environment • hello@girlplusenvironment.org

ATLANTA, GA — Across Atlanta, communities are already doing the work.

Neighbors are checking on one another during extreme heat. Community organizations are opening their doors to people who need relief. Local leaders are developing solutions that help families stay safe, lower energy burdens, and prepare for a changing climate.

On July 24, those community voices will come together on the Atlanta BeltLine to celebrate that progress and call for the protections and investments needed to continue it.

Hosted by Girl Plus Environment, the gathering will bring together residents, advocates, elected officials, and public health leaders at a moment when environmental justice programs and climate protections face growing threats at the federal level.

For years, environmental justice investments have helped communities respond to challenges that many Atlanta neighborhoods know all too well. Extreme heat, poor air quality, and rising energy costs continue to place disproportionate burdens on Black, Brown, and historically underserved communities. Local organizations and community leaders have worked to close those gaps, creating pathways to healthier, safer, and more resilient neighborhoods.

Advocates warn that recent federal actions threaten to undermine that progress.

As environmental justice programs are dismantled and environmental protections weakened, communities are being asked to confront worsening climate impacts with fewer resources. At the same time, policies that expand fossil fuel production and increase pollution threaten to intensify the very conditions communities are working to address.

“Environmental justice is about people. It is about whether families can stay safe during extreme heat. It is about whether children can breathe clean air. It is about whether communities have the resources they need to thrive. We have seen what is possible when communities are supported, and we refuse to move backwards.”

— Diamond Spratling, Founder and Executive Director, Girl Plus Environment

Speakers will connect local experiences to a broader national reality: communities cannot build resilience alone. The resources, programs, and protections that help people withstand extreme heat and environmental harm should be strengthened, not taken away.

Expected speakers include Rep. Nikema Williams, Chandra Farley, State Rep. Jazmine Clark, Dr. Jazz Nicholson, Annie Moore, and additional community leaders working on the frontlines of environmental justice in Atlanta.

Together, participants will call for continued investment in community-led solutions and reaffirm a simple truth: everyone deserves the opportunity to live, work, and thrive in a healthy environment.

About Girl Plus Environment

Girl Plus Environment is a Georgia-based nonprofit committed to educating, engaging, and empowering young Black and Brown women+ in the climate and environmental justice movements. Through organizing, advocacy, and culturally relevant programming, GPE is building a more inclusive, sustainable future from the ground up.
