Slide 02 / Executive Summary

Strategic Thesis

Instant Pot already owns household legitimacy. The proposal is to modernize how that legitimacy becomes participation, retention, and creator-native growth.

The strongest framing from the executive source deck is clear: the brand should behave like everyday infrastructure, not distant aspiration.

Everyday Infrastructure

Anchor the brand in practical utility, accessibility, and routine-led value that people recognize in their own kitchens.

From Aspiration to Participation

Shift toward comments, saves, collabs, creator systems, and social proof that reward real use over polished distance.

Retention and Ecosystems

Connect content to creator ecosystems, onboarding flows, lifecycle retention, and repeat usage rather than isolated campaign bursts.

Slide 03 / What’s Working / What’s Not

What’s Working

Strong recognition and strong utility.

  • Clear brand recognition and category familiarity.
  • Real product utility that supports education and search.
  • Pinterest fit for recipes, planning, and saves.
  • Broad creator potential because the product belongs in real homes.

What’s Not

Weak participation systems are holding growth back.

  • TikTok is weak relative to brand size.
  • Instagram remains too aspirational and detached from real kitchen use.
  • Scripted influencer content and bland presentation flatten the opportunity.
  • Weak sizing, weak continuity, and low UGC or comment participation reduce momentum.
Slide 04 / Platform Direction
TikTok
Rebuild with creator-native UGC, comments, collabs, stitches, and duets.
Instagram
Use Reels for awareness, carousels for saves, and creator collabs for continuity.
Reddit
Top priority for listening, troubleshooting, trust, and language capture.
Pinterest
Top-tier fit but underused. Lean into recipe search, planning, and save behavior.
Facebook
Redistribute short-form content and support groups plus creator collaboration.
YouTube / LinkedIn / X / Lifecycle
YouTube for explanation, LinkedIn for team and employee advocacy, X for monitoring, Email/SMS for retention.

Operating System View

Current: overly polished publishing logic with weak link-in-bio and weak participation continuity.
Future: each platform has a distinct role, but all roads lead back to search, trust, onboarding, and repeated use.
Reddit Thread UI“How do I stop getting a burn warning?” becomes product education, FAQ language, and creator content.
Pinterest Pin UI“5 dump-and-go dinners for low-energy weeknights” becomes a save loop, not a one-off post.

This platform matrix should function like a proposal operating system, not a loose content wishlist.

Slide 05 / Creator / UGC / Employee Advocacy

Current

Too many reads. Not enough real people.

  • Move away from scripted influencer reads.
  • Stop treating brand-owned publishing as the only credibility layer.
  • Reduce distance between product utility and creator voice.

Future

Build a cohort-based participation system.

  • Use creator-native prompts and collab posts on creator pages.
  • Amplify UGC with response behavior and recurring creator cohorts.
  • Develop employee recipe creators and Mila-style employee advocacy where appropriate.

Future Media Frames

Reusable placeholders for Reels, TikToks, square stills, and landscape explainers keep future production modular.

Slide 06 / KPI Layers
