This Monthly Content Engine Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between Cassandre Arkema, doing business as The Algorithm Witch ("Practitioner" or "Contractor"), and the following client organization ("Client"):

02

Scope of Services

Contractor will provide a one-month trial of content and communications support for the Client. The work is intended to function as a compact monthly content engine and may include planning, drafting, design support, coordination, and publishing preparation reasonably related to the agreed channels and priorities.

Social Content

Content creation for Instagram, Bluesky, and LinkedIn, including writing, light editing, and posting support.

Visual Posts

Infographics, carousels, and related visual assets appropriate for the monthly trial.

Event Promotion

Invitations, promotional materials, and light support tied to events or campaign moments as needed.

Newsletter Support

Newsletter drafting, editing, formatting, and/or temporary newsletter management during the trial.

Coordination

Light strategy and coordination reasonably necessary to move the agreed deliverables forward.

The exact monthly volume may be confirmed in writing by email before the trial begins. Work is scoped to a reasonable monthly volume aligned with the $1,500 USD engagement.

03

Deliverables

Deliverables may include draft and final copy, social graphics, carousel layouts, event promotional materials, newsletter copy, newsletter production support, coordination notes, and related working files needed to complete the agreed content cycle during the 30-day trial.

Deliverables will be provided in digital form using tools and file types reasonably suited to the Client's workflow. Final deliverables do not include transfer of the Contractor's private templates, internal systems, reusable frameworks, or unpublished process materials unless expressly agreed in writing.

04

Timeline and 30-Day Trial Cadence

This Agreement covers a 30-day trial period beginning on the confirmed start date and ending on the confirmed end date. During the trial, work may be delivered on a rolling basis rather than as a single batch, and the parties will coordinate by email or another agreed communication channel to confirm priorities, timing, and approvals.

05

Fees and Payment Terms

06

Upfront Payment and Deposit

$750 USD is due upfront before work begins. The upfront payment reserves the Contractor's time for the trial, covers onboarding and early production work, and is non-refundable once work has started or once the Client has approved the trial start. Work may be paused until the upfront payment is received.

07

Late Payment

The remaining $750 USD is due at the end of the 30-day trial unless the parties agree otherwise in writing. If payment is late, the Contractor may pause further work, withhold final files, or delay any extension of services until payment is brought current. The Client remains responsible for approved fees and completed work performed before any pause or termination.

08

Revisions and Out-of-Scope Work

Reasonable revisions are included for work created during the trial, provided the requests stay within the agreed scope, format, and monthly volume. Requests that materially expand the number of deliverables, add new channels, introduce major redesign work, require rush delivery, or otherwise fall outside the agreed trial may require a separate quote, a written change approval, or a future agreement.

09

Client Responsibilities

  • Provide timely access to relevant brand materials, background information, source files, approvals, and any platform or tool access needed to complete the work.
  • Designate a point of contact authorized to provide feedback and approvals during the trial.
  • Review drafts, respond to questions, and communicate decisions within a commercially reasonable timeframe.
  • Ensure any materials supplied by the Client can lawfully be used for the project, including text, photographs, logos, and third-party assets.
10

Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and is not an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer of Client. Contractor controls the manner and means of performing the services, subject to the agreed scope, timeline, and deliverables. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship or gives either party authority to bind the other.

11

Taxes

Contractor is responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from payments received under this Agreement. Client will not withhold payroll taxes, provide employee benefits, or treat Contractor as an employee. Fees are stated in United States Dollars unless otherwise agreed in writing.

12

Intellectual Property

After full payment is received, Client receives ownership of the final approved deliverables created specifically for Client under this Agreement. Contractor retains ownership of pre-existing materials, templates, strategy frameworks, working files, processes, know-how, and reusable systems. Contractor may reuse general knowledge and non-confidential methods developed during the engagement.

Contractor may reference the work in a portfolio, case study, or professional materials unless Client requests confidentiality in writing.

13

Portfolio Use

Unless the Client requests otherwise in writing for confidential or sensitive work, the Contractor may reference the Client's name and display non-confidential final deliverables in the Contractor's portfolio, website, case studies, proposals, or social channels for the limited purpose of showcasing completed work. The Contractor will not disclose confidential strategy, unpublished materials, or private operating details without permission.

14

Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep the other party's non-public business, operational, financial, and strategic information confidential and to use that information only as needed to perform or evaluate this Agreement. This obligation does not apply to information that is already public through no fault of the receiving party, was lawfully known before disclosure, or must be disclosed by law.

15

Cancellation and Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement by written notice. If the Client terminates after work has begun, the upfront payment remains non-refundable and the Client remains responsible for payment for approved work completed through the termination date. If the Contractor terminates without cause before completing the trial, the Contractor will deliver any completed work prepared to date and discuss a fair prorated adjustment if appropriate.

16

Limitation of Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Contractor’s total liability under this Agreement is limited to the amount actually paid by Client under this Agreement. Contractor is not liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, punitive, or lost-profit damages.

17

Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Illinois, United States, without regard to conflict of law principles. The parties agree to work in good faith to resolve any dispute informally before pursuing formal remedies.
