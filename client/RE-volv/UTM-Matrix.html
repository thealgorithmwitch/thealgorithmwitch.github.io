UTM Matrix
Deployment Guide

> 00. Purpose

Create tracking links that keep campaign attribution clean in GA4, match the campaign registry, support dashboard reporting, and reduce unattributed or fragmented traffic.

This supports the project goal of building a trusted data foundation and a standardized "Link Tracking Calculator" for future campaign use.

01

When to Create a UTM Link

Required Scenarios

  • Email Sends
  • Social Posts (Organic & Paid)
  • Partner & Press Links
  • QR Codes
  • Campaign Landing Page Promotion

Prohibited Scenarios

  • Internal Navigation
  • Links only used inside the website
  • Unplanned copy/paste links
02

Required Inputs Before You Start

Before opening the UTM Matrix, ensure the following structure is defined. This follows the tracking-first logic: define the structure before launch so data flows into the registry cleanly.

FIELD 01 Destination URL
FIELD 02 Source & Medium
FIELD 03 Campaign Name
FIELD 04 Primary Goal
03

Required UTM Fields

Parameter Definition Examples
utm_source Where the traffic comes from. google, linkedin, facebook, instagram, hs_email, newsletter, partner
utm_medium The type of distribution or channel. cpc, paid_social, organic_social, email, referral, qr, press, partnership
utm_campaign The normalized name of the initiative. solar_story_series, ribbon_cutting_april2026, donor_newsletter_launch
utm_content Optional: variant, asset, or placement. cta_button, banner_blue, welcome_table
04

Naming Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Source Standard

GOOD: linkedin, instagram, google
AVOID: LinkedIn, IG, Google Ads

Medium Standard

GOOD: email, cpc, paid_social
AVOID: social-paid, paidsocial, e-mail

Campaign Standard

GOOD: solar_story_series
AVOID: Solar Story Series!
Rule: Lowercase standardized names only. No spaces. Underscores instead of punctuation. Inconsistent naming fragments reporting.
05

Workflow for Creating a New Link

01

Confirm Destination URL

Use the final public URL. Ensure it's live and supports the intended conversion action.

02

Open the UTM Matrix

Navigate to the UTM Matrix section in your dashboard workspace.

03

Enter Required Fields

Populate Destination URL, Source, Medium, and Campaign Name.

04

Add Optional Details

Use utm_content for variants or utm_term for keywords when granular reporting is needed.

05

Review Link Integrity

Confirm validity, check for typos, and ensure naming matches standards before proceeding.

06

Save to Registry

CRITICAL: Save the link to the registry so it becomes part of the tracked system. Prevents audit flags.

09

QA Checklist Before Publishing

Checkpoint Pass Condition
Destination URL Link opens correctly and lander supports intended CTA.
Standardization Source, Medium, and Campaign follow lowercase/underscore rules.
Registry Sync Link has been saved to registry; deployed version matches exactly.
No Special Chars No spaces or odd punctuation (!, @, #) in campaign name.
10

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Manual Editing

Do not hand-edit UTM strings after generation. If a variant is needed, create a new entry.

Casual Labels

Do not invent new medium labels casually. Use only the standardized set in the SOP.

Capitalization

Avoid mixed case. 'Google' and 'google' report as two separate sources in GA4.

Renaming

Do not rename campaigns mid-stream. It breaks historical reporting comparison.

12

Ownership & Responsibility

Comms / Campaign Owner

  • Choosing correct source, medium, and campaign
  • Deploying the right saved link
  • Requesting new links for new variants

Analytics / Consultant

  • Maintaining naming standards
  • Reviewing registry cleanliness
  • Flagging fragmented naming & validating behavior

The Final Rule

Do not publish a campaign link until it has been generated in the UTM Matrix, reviewed for naming consistency, saved to the registry, and copied exactly into the live placement.
